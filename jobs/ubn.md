For having worked more than a few years in this company I have taken on several roles. Originally entered as an intern digital communications I quickly noticed that the company didn't offer a very good digital experience. I set up a digital infrastructure across Google's products and the popular social media. After that, I stayed aboard as a fulltime employee to work on the candidate and customer experience.

Over the years I have been working as the main digital innovator in a fast growing company, and I have done various things to help facilitate this growth. UBN has grown from circa 30 people to over 80 in this period.

Some of the most important things I have worked on are;

- System migration to Mysolution (Hybrid SF / NAV SaaS supplier)  
  Basically all processes were contained in an oracle-based SaaS supplier for the temp agency market. Starting from the website's application form to the payment slip that the temp receives and the tax declarations done to the government. The software we were using for this was starting to show it's age, costing more and more time and blocking further growth. However, switching providers is an intense move, with a lot of critical risks involved. A colleague and me did the initial scouting and found a provider that would suit us. We managed to convince internal stakeholders that this was the right move. Together with a third colleague we led the project that within half a year would have to result in a completely new proccess from A to Z. I was primarily responsible for the frontoffice proccesses (the processes that make up about 80% of internal work load, basically everything excluding the financial stuff), external connections (the website and other external connections) and business intelligence (reporting on commercial, financial and operational insights). It was hard work, but the result is to be proud of.
- (2018) New website  
  When the SaaS-supplier allowed us to pick a website provider freely I ran the migration. I worked together with the (then) new marketing colleague, who did most of the visual identity and text. I took over the technical part, including integrations. I operated in the role of customer, but used my in-depth understanding of tech to make sure the project ran (almost) flawless.
